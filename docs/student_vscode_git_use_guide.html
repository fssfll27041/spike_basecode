Steps 1 – 5 · Open, Code, and Deploy
1
Open VS Code
Find VS Code on the laptop — it's the blue swirly icon. Click it to open. The team's project folder should already be loaded. If not, ask your coach to help open spike_basecode.
2
Find Your Mission File
In the file panel on the left side, look for your mission — something like mission_01.py. Click it to open it in the editor. That's your file to work on today!
Curious about robot.py? That's the robot's brain! Take a peek — and talk to your coach if you have ideas to improve it. 🤓
3
Write Your Code
Start typing! VS Code shows suggestions as you go and draws red squiggly lines under mistakes. Hover over a squiggle to see what's wrong.

🏗️ Engineer's tip: Start small. Get one thing working first, then add more. A robot that reliably does one thing beats one that tries five things and crashes!

4
Power On Your SPIKE Hub
Press the big center button on the robot's brain to turn it on. Wait for it to show your robot's name on screen. It connects to the laptop wirelessly over Bluetooth — no cables!
5
Send Your Code to the Robot
Click the Run and Debug icon in the left sidebar (looks like a ▷ with a bug). Use the dropdown at the top to pick your robot's name. Then click the big green ▶ Play button — your code flies over to the robot!

4

Steps 6 – 9 · Repeat and Save Your Work
6
Watch, Adjust, and Run Again
Watch carefully what the robot does. Go too far? Wrong direction? Head back to VS Code, fix the code, then click the green ▶ Play button again — every click sends your freshest changes to the robot. Repeat until it does exactly what you want!

🏆 This is real engineering. Professional robotics teams do this exact loop every day. You're doing it the right way.

End of Session · Save Your Work

Before you close the laptop, lock in your progress. This is called a commit — like saving your game, but it also remembers exactly what you changed and why.

7
Open GitHub Desktop
Find GitHub Desktop on the laptop — it has a white Octocat circle icon 🐙. Open it. You'll see a list of files you changed on the left side.
8
Write a Commit Message
In the Summary box at the bottom-left, describe what you did — like a subject line for an email. Be specific! Future you (and your teammates) will be glad you did.

9
Click "Commit to main"
Click the green button. Your work is saved to the team's local code history. That's your part done — great work!
You did it! Your code is saved. 🎉 Nice work, engineer!

☁️ What about pushing to GitHub? Your coach will push the code to the cloud from home later, where there's wi-fi. Your commit is safe — they'll take it from here!

🔬 Want to go deeper?
Once your mission is working reliably, take a peek at robot.py — it's the shared code that makes everything run. You might spot something to improve or a feature you'd love to add. Talk to your coach and explore together. The best engineers are always curious!
